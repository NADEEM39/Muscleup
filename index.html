EXERCISE DIRECTORY

THE ULTIMATE MUSCLE GROUP GUIDE & HOW TO BEST TRAIN THEM

The human body has 650+ distinct muscles, but you don’t need to memorize them all to master weight training.

Sometimes less is more. That’s why we’re simplifying things to just 11 easy-to-remember muscle groups.

You’ll also find the best tips for each muscle group–derived from cutting-edge science, world-class coaches, and practical real-world experience.

Follow the advice laid out in this article to achieve a fit, balanced physique while reducing the risk of injury.

ABS

The term "abs" refers to the abdominal muscles, which are a group of muscles located in the front of the abdomen. The major muscles in this group include the rectus abdominis (commonly known as the "six-pack" muscle), the external obliques, and the internal obliques. These muscles play a crucial role in maintaining core stability and supporting various movements of the trunk.

The Best Ab Exercises:

(A) Crunches:

  • 1.Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the floor.
  • 2.Place your hands behind your head or cross them over your chest
  • 3.Engage your core and lift your upper back off the ground, curling your shoulders towards your hips
  • 4.Exhale as you crunch and inhale as you lower back down.
  • 5.Avoid pulling on your neck and focus on using your abdominal muscles to lift.

    • (B) Planks:

  • 1.Start in a push-up position with your hands directly under your shoulders and toes on the ground.
  • 2.Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels, engaging your core and glutes.
  • 3.Hold this position, making sure your hips don't sag or rise.
  • 4.Aim to maintain the plank for as long as you can with proper form.

    • (C) Leg Raises:

  • 1.Lie on your back with your legs extended and your hands under your hips or by your sides.
  • 2.Keep your lower back pressed into the floor as you lift both legs off the ground.
  • 3.Raise your legs until they are perpendicular to the floor or slightly lower if needed.
  • 4.Slowly lower your legs back down without letting them touch the floor.
    • BICEPS

    The biceps brachii, commonly referred to as the biceps, is a muscle located in the upper arm. It consists of two "heads" or points of origin: the short head and the long head. The short head originates from the coracoid process of the scapula (shoulder blade), while the long head originates from the supraglenoid tubercle, a part of the scapula near the shoulder joint.

    The Best Biceps Exercises:

    (A) Barbell Bicep Curl:

  • 1.Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and grip a barbell with an underhand grip (palms facing up) that is slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  • 2.Keep your elbows close to your sides and curl the barbell towards your shoulders while keeping your upper arms stationary.
  • 3.Lower the barbell back down in a controlled manner, fully extending your elbows.
  • 4.This exercise targets both the short and long heads of the biceps.

    • (B) Preacher Curl:

  • 1.Use a preacher curl bench or an inclined bench at a slight angle.
  • 2.Rest your upper arms on the bench, holding an EZ barbell with an underhand grip.
  • 3.Curl the barbell up while keeping your upper arms stationary on the bench.
  • 4.Lower the barbell back down in a controlled manner.

    • (C) Hammer Curl:

  • 1.Hold a dumbbell in each hand with a neutral grip (palms facing each other).
  • 2.Curl the dumbbells towards your shoulders while keeping your palms facing each other throughout the movement.
  • 3.Lower the dumbbells back down in a controlled manner.
  • 4.Hammer curls target the brachialis muscle in addition to the biceps.
    • CHEST

    The chest muscles are known as the pectoralis muscles, commonly referred to as "pecs."

    The pectoralis major is the primary muscle in the chest and is responsible for various movements of the shoulder joint.

    The muscle has two parts: the clavicular head (upper chest) and the sternal head (lower chest), which connect to the collarbone and breastbone respectively.

    The Best Chest Exercises:

    (A)Bench Press:

  • 1.Lie on a flat bench with a barbell or dumbbells. If using a barbell, grip it slightly wider than shoulder-width with an overhand grip.
  • 2.Lower the barbell or dumbbells to your chest while keeping your elbows at a 45-degree angle.
  • 3.Press the weight back up to the starting position, fully extending your arms.
  • 4.Bench presses are a classic compound movement that primarily targets the middle and lower chest, as well as the triceps and shoulders.

    • (B) Incline Dumbbell Press:

  • 1.Set an adjustable bench at a 30-45 degree incline.
  • 2.Hold a dumbbell in each hand and lie back on the bench.
  • 3.Lower the dumbbells to the sides of your upper chest, then press them back up.
  • 4.Incline presses target the upper chest and shoulders more than the flat bench press.

    • (C) Cable Crossover:

  • 1.Set up two cable pulleys at shoulder height.
  • 2.Hold one handle in each hand and stand in the center.
  • 3.Step forward slightly and slightly bend your elbows.
  • 4.Bring your hands together in front of your chest in a hugging motion.
  • 5.Cable crossovers provide constant tension on the chest muscles throughout the movement.
    • Calves

    The calf muscles, collectively known as the triceps surae, consist of two main muscles: the gastrocnemius and the soleus.

    The gastrocnemius is the larger, more visible muscle and has two heads. It originates above the knee and inserts into the Achilles tendon.

    The soleus is located underneath the gastrocnemius and has a wider distribution, originating from the tibia and fibula bones.

    The Best Calve Exercises:

    (A)Seated Calf Raises:

  • 1.Sit on a calf raise machine or a bench with a weighted bar or dumbbells across your thighs.
  • 2.Place the balls of your feet on the edge of a raised platform, and let your heels drop down.
  • 3.Push your heels up as high as possible by contracting your calf muscles.
  • 4.Lower your heels back down to get a stretch in your calves.
  • 5.Seated calf raises emphasize the soleus, a deeper calf muscle, and also engage the gastrocnemius.

    • (B) Calf Raises on Leg Press Machine:

  • 1.Use a leg press machine with a calf raise attachment or a sturdy block.
  • 2.Place the balls of your feet on the platform and your heels hanging off.
  • 3.Push the platform up by extending your ankles and contracting your calf muscles.
  • 4.Lower your heels to get a stretch in your calves.
  • 5.his variation also effectively targets both the gastrocnemius and soleus muscles.

    • (C) Jump Rope Skipping:

  • 1.Jumping rope is a dynamic and cardiovascular exercise that engages the calf muscles.
  • 2.Jump on the balls of your feet, using your calves to propel yourself off the ground.
  • 3.As you jump, focus on the calf contraction and the quick ankle movement.
  • 4.Jump rope is not only a great calf exercise but also a fun and effective way to improve overall fitness.
    • SHOILDERS

    The shoulder is a complex joint that consists of multiple muscles and tendons.

    The main muscle groups of the shoulders include the deltoids, which have three heads: anterior (front), lateral (side), and posterior (rear).

    Other muscles like the rotator cuff muscles and the trapezius also play important roles in shoulder movement and stability.

    The Best Shoulder Exercises:

    (A)Overhead Press (Barbell or Dumbbell):

  • 1.Stand or sit with a barbell or dumbbells at shoulder level.
  • 2.Press the weight overhead while extending your arms.
  • 3.Lower the weight back down to shoulder level.
  • 4.Overhead presses target the entire shoulder complex, with an emphasis on the anterior (front) deltoids.

    • (B) Lateral Raises:

  • 1.Hold a dumbbell in each hand by your sides.
  • 2.Lift the dumbbells out to the sides until they are at shoulder level.
  • 3.Lower the dumbbells back down in a controlled manner.
  • 4.Lateral raises primarily target the lateral (side) deltoids and help widen the shoulders.

    • (C) Rear Delt Flyes:

  • 1.Lie face down on an incline bench, holding dumbbells in each hand.
  • 2.Start with the dumbbells hanging straight down and raise them out to the sides.
  • 3.Squeeze your shoulder blades together as you lift.
  • 4.Rear delt flyes target the posterior (rear) deltoids and help balance shoulder development.
    • GLUTES

    The glutes, or gluteal muscles, are a group of three muscles located in the buttocks: the gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, and gluteus minimus.

    The gluteus maximus is the largest muscle in the body and is responsible for hip extension, which involves moving the thigh backward. It also plays a role in hip external rotation and contributes to overall hip stability.

    The Best Glutes Exercises:

    (A)Squats:

  • 1.Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart.
  • 2.Lower your hips down and back as if sitting into an imaginary chair, keeping your chest up and knees aligned with your toes.
  • 3.Push through your heels to stand back up.
  • 4.Squats engage the glutes, quadriceps, and hamstrings, promoting overall lower body strength.

    • (B) Hip Thrusts:

  • 1.Sit on the ground with your upper back against a bench or platform.
  • 2.Roll a barbell or place a weight plate over your hips.
  • 3.Press through your heels to lift your hips off the ground, creating a straight line from your shoulders to your knees.
  • 4.Squeeze your glutes at the top and lower your hips back down.

    • (C)Glute Bridges:

  • 1.Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground.
  • 2.Lift your hips off the ground while pushing through your heels, creating a straight line from your shoulders to your knees.
  • 3.Squeeze your glutes at the top and lower your hips back down.
  • 4.Glute bridges are an excellent isolation exercise for targeting the glutes and improving hip extension strength.
    • TERES

    TThe teres muscles are two small muscles located in the upper back and shoulder region. They are known as the teres major and teres minor.

    The teres major is a larger muscle that runs along the lateral border of the scapula (shoulder blade) and attaches to the humerus bone in the upper arm.

    The teres minor is a smaller muscle located just above the teres major. It also attaches to the scapula and inserts into the humerus.

    The Best Teres Exercises:

    (A)Rows (Various Types):

  • Exercises like bent-over rows, seated rows, and cable rows engage the muscles of the upper back, which can include the teres major and teres minor. Focus on squeezing your shoulder blades together during the pulling motion to activate these muscles.

    • (B) Lat Pulldowns:

  • Lat pulldowns primarily target the latissimus dorsi muscles but also engage the muscles of the upper back, including the teres muscles. Ensure proper scapular retraction during the movement to involve the teres muscles more effectively.

    • (C)External Rotation Exercises:

  • External rotation exercises, often performed with resistance bands or light dumbbells, can engage the teres minor, which is involved in external rotation of the arm. Examples include lying external rotations and standing band external rotations.

    • (D)Face Pulls:

  • Face pulls with a rope attachment on a cable machine can help engage the rear deltoids, traps, and other upper back muscles, potentially including the teres muscles. Focus on pulling the rope towards your face while keeping your elbows high.
    • TRICEPS

    The triceps brachii, commonly referred to as the triceps, is a muscle located on the back of the upper arm.

    It consists of three heads: the lateral head, medial head, and long head. These heads have different origins but share a common insertion point at the olecranon process of the ulna bone.

    The Best Triceps Exercises:

    (A)Close-Grip Bench Press:

  • 1.Lie on a flat bench and grip the barbell with your hands closer than shoulder-width apart.
  • 2.Lower the barbell to your chest while keeping your elbows close to your body.
  • 3.Push the barbell back up to the starting position, fully extending your arms.
  • 4.Close-grip bench press targets the triceps while also engaging the chest and shoulders.

    • (B)Skull Crushers (Lying Tricep Extensions):

  • 1.Lie on a bench and hold a barbell or dumbbells above your forehead.
  • 2.Bend your elbows to lower the weight towards your forehead while keeping your upper arms stationary.
  • 3.Extend your elbows to lift the weight back up.
  • 4.Skull crushers isolate the triceps and can be performed with various grip widths.

    • (C)Tricep Rope Pushdowns:

  • 1.Attach a rope handle to a cable machine at the high position.
  • 2.Hold the rope with an overhand grip and extend your elbows to push the rope down.
  • 3.Squeeze your triceps at the bottom of the movement.
  • 4.Tricep rope pushdowns target the lateral head of the triceps.
